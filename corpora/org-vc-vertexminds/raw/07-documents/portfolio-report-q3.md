## Company 2: FitBrew — D2C Supplements

### Business Overview
FitBrew is a direct-to-consumer (DTC) supplement e-commerce brand selling protein powders, vitamins, and fitness nutrition products. Products sourced/manufactured via partners; FitBrew handles branding, marketing, fulfillment, customer acquisition.

**Founding Team:** Former fitness influencers; strong social media presence; limited operational/finance background.
**Funding History:** Pre-seed round ($600K), deployed primarily into inventory and marketing.
**Market Position:** Crowded fitness supplement space; competing with legacy brands (Myprotein, Optimum Nutrition) and DTC entrants.

### Q3 2025 Performance — Detailed

#### Revenue Metrics
| Metric | July | August | September | Q3 Total |
|--------|------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **MRR** | €4.8K | €4.6K | €4.2K | €4.5K avg |
| **Customers (monthly active)** | 680 | 620 | 590 | — |
| **Orders** | 285 | 265 | 245 | 795 |
| **AOV (Average Order Value)** | €16.84 | €17.36 | €17.12 | €17.1 avg |
| **Repeat Purchase Rate** | 28% | 24% | 21% | — |

**Growth Trajectory:** **NEGATIVE.** MRR down 12.5% QoQ (€4.8K → €4.2K). Customer count down 13% (680 → 590). Repeat purchase rate collapsing (28% → 21%, a 25% decline). This is concerning.

#### Unit Economics — The Core Problem
| Metric | Q2 (Reference) | Q3 2025 | Direction | Analysis |
|--------|-------|---|---|---|
| **CAC (blended paid channels)** | €52 | €65 | ↑ **Deteriorating** | Instagram/TikTok CPM rising; founders still relying on paid ads |
| **LTV (12-month cohort basis)** | €204 | €142 | ↓ **Deteriorating** | Rising churn + lower repeat rate = shorter customer lifetime |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | 3.9x | 2.2x | ↓ **Critical** | Below 3x is unsustainable; at 2.2x, unit economics broken |
| **Payback Period** | 2.4 months | 4.7 months | ↑ **Longer** | Payback doubled; capital stuck longer |
| **Gross Margin** | 62% | 60% | ↓ Slight decline | Product cost increasing (freight, manufacturing) |
| **Monthly Churn** | 11.2% | 14.3% | ↑ **Worsening** | Customers staying shorter; repeat rate declining |

**Root Cause Analysis:**

The data points to a classic DTC failure pattern:

1. **Customer Acquisition Cost Rising (€52 → €65):** FitBrew is still acquiring customers primarily via paid social (Instagram, TikTok). As the team scales spending, CPM (cost per thousand impressions) rises. Founders' organic/influencer reach has plateaued.

2. **Customer Lifetime Value Declining (€204 → €142):** This is driven by two factors:
   - **Churn increasing:** Monthly churn rose from 11.2% to 14.3%. Customers are leaving faster.
   - **Repeat purchase rate collapsing:** From 28% → 21%. This suggests product-market fit concerns — customers buy once but don't return.

3. **Cohort Analysis (implied):** Early cohorts (pre-Q2) had higher retention because the brand was novel. Recent cohorts (Q3) acquired at higher CAC churn faster — indicating quality of acquired customers declining.

**Result:** Unit economics are **underwater.**
- LTV:CAC of 2.2x means for every €1 spent on customer acquisition, the company generates €2.20 in lifetime value.
- After COGS (40% of revenue), operating costs, and other expenses, this is **unprofitable**.
- At current burn rate (see below), runway is limited.

#### Financial Health
| Item | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Monthly Burn Rate (est.)** | €6.8K | Includes: COGS (~€1.8K), fulfillment/logistics (~€1.2K), team (~€2K), marketing (~€1.8K) |
| **Cash on Hand (Sept 30)** | €72K | Down from €110K at June 30 (€38K burned Q3) |
| **Runway (at current burn)** | 10.6 months | To zero; but company will be unprofitable long before |
| **Break-Even Timeline (at current trajectory)** | **Not achievable without intervention** | Losing money on unit economics |

**Cash Burn Detail:**
- Q3 burned €38K (€12.7K/month average)
- If trend continues, runway is ~5.7 months to zero cash
- **But:** Company cannot sustain this burn; founders will be forced to:
  - Cut marketing spend (CAC rise driven by scaling, but need to pull back)
  - Seek bridge financing
  - Significantly restructure business model

### Root Cause: The "Growth at All Costs" Trap
FitBrew's founding team are effective marketers/influencers but lack operational discipline. In Q2/Q3, they scaled marketing spend aggressively (driving CAC up) expecting revenue to follow. Instead:
- **Organic reach plateaued:** Influencer followers aren't infinite; paid ads become only channel
- **Market saturation:** Supplement market is crowded; CAC naturally rises as competition increases
- **Product-market fit not proven:** 21% repeat rate is low; suggests customers are trying product, not loving it
- **No unit economics discipline:** Team continued scaling CAC spend without validating that LTV could justify it

### Red Flags Noted in Q3
1. ✗ LTV:CAC dropped below 3x (unsustainable threshold)
2. ✗ Churn accelerating (11% → 14%/month)
3. ✗ Repeat purchase rate collapsing (28% → 21%)
4. ✗ CAC rising while customer quality declining
5. ✗ No clear path to improve unit economics without operational changes

### Required Interventions

**Immediate (October 2025):**
- [ ] Founder check-in call (scheduled Nov 20, but should move earlier)
- [ ] Detailed cohort analysis: understand which acquisition channels deliver retention, which don't
- [ ] Marketing spend audit: identify lowest-CAC, highest-LTV channels
- [ ] Product feedback: conduct customer interviews on repeat purchase barriers

**Medium-term (Q4 2025):**
- **Option A (Preferred):** Shift marketing to organic/retention channels (email, SMS, affiliate partnerships, micro-influencer seeding). Cut paid spend to sustainable level (€1.2K/month). Focus on repeat purchase optimization (product improvements, loyalty program, sampling).
- **Option B (If Option A fails):** Seek bridge financing (€100–150K) to extend runway while pivoting. Less preferable; signals loss of investor confidence.
- **Option C (Last resort):** Merge/acquihire or wind down. Company is not approaching profitability path without major changes.

**Success Criteria for Intervention:**
- LTV:CAC improves to 3.5x+ by Q1 2026
- Repeat purchase rate stabilizes at 24%+ (current 21% is untenable)
- CAC holds flat or declines as organic channels scale
- Monthly churn improves to <12%

### Risk Assessment: HIGH
**Confidence Level:** Moderate–High
**Urgency:** Critical (intervention needed now, not Q4)

## Lessons for Demo Day Alpha Decisions

Vertex Minds' experience with FitBrew directly informed the IC's skepticism during Demo Day Alpha (Nov 12, 2025) when evaluating GreenLoop and DataPulse:

### Lesson 1: GMV/Revenue ≠ Business Health
- **FitBrew Parallel:** Q3 2025, the company had €35K in Q3 commission revenue (€4.2K MRR). This looks "okay" in isolation, but unit economics were broken.
- **Applied to GreenLoop:** GreenLoop pitch highlighted €180K GMV in 8 weeks (€14.4K revenue). But unit-level economics not proven; repeat buyer rate low (28%). IC was appropriately skeptical.
- **Applied to DataPulse:** €8K MRR from 2 customers sounded good until we examined CAC (€6.2K per customer) against 2-customer retention curve (too small to validate). Concern: revenue growth masking deteriorating unit economics, similar to FitBrew pattern.

### Lesson 2: Unit Economics Must Be Validated Before Scaling
- **FitBrew Failure Mode:** Founders scaled marketing spend (visible driver of revenue growth) without validating that CAC could be recovered. CAC rose as they scaled; LTV didn't follow. Classic DTC trap.
- **Applied to GreenLoop:** Marketplace model has similar risk. Both supply and demand must scale together. If GreenLoop scales supply-side first without demand, unit margins compress (higher holding costs, lower fill rates). IC was right to pass.

### Lesson 3: Churn & Retention Trends Matter More Than Cohort Snapshots
- **FitBrew Signal:** The 28% → 21% repeat purchase rate decline is the canary in the coal mine. Early cohorts looked fine (28% repeat); recent cohorts worse (21%). This cohort degradation suggests acquisition quality declining.
- **Applied to DataPulse:** Only 2 customers means we can't validate retention curve. IC appropriately noted this as risk. If 2 customers churn in next quarter, MRR drops 50%. No visibility into stable retention.

### Lesson 4: Founder Discipline & Operational Excellence Predict Outcomes
- **FitBrew Contrast:** NestEase (Performing company) has disciplined founders tracking CAC, LTV, payback period meticulously. FitBrew founders are excellent marketers but lack operational discipline; no evidence they tracked unit economics until Q3 problems emerged.
- **Applied to Demo Day:** IC favored NovaTech and UrbanFlow partly because founders demonstrated operational rigor (traction data is precise, roadmap is realistic, unit economics are tracked). MindBridge and DataPulse founders are talented but lack evidence of operational discipline at scale.
