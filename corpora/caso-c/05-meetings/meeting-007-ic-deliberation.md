## NOTES & OBSERVATIONS

1. **Pattern identified and acknowledged**: Daniel tends to prioritize founder personality and product elegance over numerical rigor (example: FitBrew pattern). Ava's push-back was direct and appropriate. Daniel accepted the feedback.

2. **DataPulse failure mode**: This case exemplified the broader risk that founder optimism and metric obfuscation are correlated. The inability to reconcile 12,000 users (50K claimed vs 38K verified, 24% of claimed base) raised existential questions about entire unit economics model and founder credibility.

3. **UrbanFlow investment decision**: Initial skepticism about "logistics business" was successfully reframed as "software-layer business with logistics wedge." INVEST with terms (€180K, second hub milestone within 9 months, mandatory IP review, 45-day close). European market data gap remains a real unknown, but mitigated by conditional milestones and IP verification.

4. **MindBridge: Conditional as risk-layering tool**: Tomás's structure (warrant-based, no board seat until conditions met, escrow) turned a "probably PASS" into a "yes with explicit risk gates." This is the right use of CONDITIONAL — not "we're not sure yet," but "we're interested if you can prove X."

5. **NovaTech's technical evaluation ceiling**: Honest acknowledgment that VC team cannot validate NLP benchmarks. Proper workaround: invest on product-market fit + team credibility, document the limitation. This is appropriate risk management, not a red flag.

6. **Fatigue noted at 45-minute mark**: Energy dip around 09:45. Five-minute break restored focus. Decisions after break (MindBridge onward) remained crisp; earlier decisions (DataPulse, UrbanFlow) also benefited from having fresh context.

7. **Unresolved items**: Reference check on Yara and UrbanFlow IP review remain as open TODOs. These are not show-stoppers (we've committed to closing dates), but they are still items in the queue that require follow-through.
