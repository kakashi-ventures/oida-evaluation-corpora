## Slide 2: The Problem
Urban last-mile delivery is broken:
- Van-based delivery creates congestion, emissions, parking chaos in city centers
- Retailers pay €8–15 per delivery for city logistics (low margin for merchants)
- City air quality: 40% of urban CO2 emissions from delivery vehicles
- Retailers have no choice: centralized logistics networks can't serve density efficiently

**Market Size:** €18B annual logistics spend for urban retail in EU; 30% margin opportunity via micro-logistics.

---

## Slide 3: The Solution
UrbanFlow operates a fleet of electric cargo bikes for same-day delivery in city centers:

**Service:**
- Pick up from retailer warehouses (urban periphery) every 2–4 hours
- Deliver to customers within city (0–8km radius, 30–90 minutes)
- Weather-proof cargo capacity: 40–80kg per bike
- Real-time tracking (customer + retailer visibility)
- Insured, licensed riders

**Economics:**
- Cost per delivery: €3.20 (vs. €8–12 for van)
- Delivers 12–18 parcels per day per bike
- Carbon: 0g CO2 per delivery (vs. 2.1kg van average)

## Slide 13: Regulatory & City Environment
- **Bike Lane Infrastructure:** All target cities have established bike lane networks (EU trend toward 30km/hour zones)
- **Regulatory Status:** Cargo e-bikes classified as bicycles (not motor vehicles) in most EU jurisdictions; minimal licensing overhead
- **Insurance:** Commercial liability included; city-specific permits in progress (€800–2K per city)
- **Expansion Barrier:** Low (bikes are legal, model scales without new tech). Competitive moat: customer relationships + rider quality + brand.

## Slide 14: Risk Mitigation
| Risk | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| Weather impact on utilization | Covered cargo design; surge pricing model for high-demand periods; rider flexibility (gig-based incentives) |
| Rider churn | Competitive wage (€18/hr, above-market); flexible scheduling; community culture (regular social events, branded gear) |
| Regulatory change | Monitor city policies; proactive engagement with municipal transportation authorities |
| Scale challenges (operations, safety) | Phased city expansion (master one city before moving); hire dedicated safety/compliance lead by Q1 |
