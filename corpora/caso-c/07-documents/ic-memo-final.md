## Decision Rationale — Detailed

### 1. NovaTech AI (INVEST)
**Founders:** Marco Bianchi (CEO), Yara Osman (CTO)
**Ask:** €200K at 8%, pre-money €2.3M
**Decision:** GREEN LIGHT

**Scoring Summary:**
- Product-Market Fit: 8/10 (12 beta customers, 3 paying, NRR 140%, zero churn early)
- Team: 8/10 (Bianchi: strong architectural tech background; Osman: credible ML/NLP track record)
- Market Size: 7/10 (€3.4B TAM stated; note: pitch deck TAM differs from analyst dossier — pitch-deck figure likely conservative extrapolation)
- Business Model: 8/10 (SaaS, 3.2-month payback, LTV:CAC 6.7x — strong unit economics)
- Execution Risk: Low (focused product, clear roadmap, capital efficient)

**Rationale:**
- Strongest traction in cohort. Three paying customers in week 3 is exceptional for B2B SaaS.
- NRR 140% (expansion from existing pilots) signals strong product-market satisfaction.
- Team complementarity is clear: Bianchi (industry expertise + founder experience), Osman (technical depth).
- Capital efficient: €200K runway extends 8 months given €28K burn; targets profitability Q3 2026.
- Market tailwinds: EU regulatory compliance requirements driving demand for documentation automation.
- Risk mitigated by: Low-code implementation strategy (Revit integrations, modular compliance packs reduce dependency on external partners).

**Investment Terms:**
- Check size: €200K (Series Seed)
- Equity: 8%
- No board seat requested; quarterly updates expected

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Reference checks: Contact pilot customers for satisfaction verification

### 2. GreenLoop (PASS)
**Founders:** Luca Ferretti (CEO), Stefan Berger (COO)
**Ask:** €400K at 6%, pre-money €6.3M
**Decision:** DECLINE

**Scoring Summary:**
- Product-Market Fit: 6/10 (€180K GMV in 8 weeks = decent traction, but low repeat buyer rate at 28%)
- Team: 6.5/10 (Ferretti: construction industry experience; Berger: ops solid; but limited founder/scale-up track record)
- Market Size: 7/10 (€14B TAM, real problem, but fragmented supply chain = execution complexity)
- Business Model: 5.5/10 (8% take rate on €180K GMV = €14.4K revenue; runway only 6 months; unit economics not yet proven)
- Execution Risk: High (marketplace requires both supply and demand; currently supply-side heavy)

**Rationale:**
- Core issue: Marketplaces require liquidity on both sides. GreenLoop has supplier inventory but buyer adoption is slow (28% repeat rate suggests low buyer affinity).
- Ask of €400K at 6% values at €6.3M pre-money (likely 3x overvalued given traction level).
- GMV obscures unit economics: €14.4K revenue /€180K GMV is thin margin with significant operational costs (logistics coordination, QA).
- Team lacks demonstrated marketplace scaling experience (Ferretti/Berger have construction + ops but not growth/distribution chops).
- Timeline concern: 6-month runway insufficient for marketplace to achieve critical mass; capital raise would need to extend well into 2026.
- Risk: Competitor entry risk is real (larger construction tech firms could bolt marketplace onto existing platforms).

**Feedback Provided:**
- Encourage team to focus on supply-side stickiness first (increase repeat GMV per supplier).
- Refine unit economics before next raise (should target >12% contribution margin).
- Consider partnership strategy with construction software platforms (ERP, project management) for distribution.
- Reapproach in 6 months with stronger metrics.

**Decision Made:** Polite pass letter sent Nov 13, 2025.

### 3. MindBridge (CONDITIONAL)
**Founders:** Anna Kowalski (CEO), Raj Patel (CTO)
**Ask:** €150K at 10%, pre-money €1.35M
**Decision:** CONDITIONAL INTEREST

**Scoring Summary:**
- Product-Market Fit: 7/10 (3 pilots = early validation; 0 revenue but strong feedback)
- Team: 8/10 (Kowalski: 10 years HR tech + founder track record; Patel: solid ML credentials)
- Market Size: 8/10 (€8.2B TAM, real problem, evergreen demand)
- Business Model: 6/10 (PEPM model is proven in HR-tech; but 72% accuracy on pilot data is unvalidated at scale; enterprise sales cycles long)
- Execution Risk: Medium (model accuracy + sales cycle are unknowns)

**Rationale for Conditional:**
- Strong founders, large market, reasonable ask (€150K at 10% is conservative vs. NovaTech).
- However, **zero revenue** is material risk. Pilots are not paying; pilot companies have not committed to post-pilot contracts.
- ML accuracy at 72% on small pilot dataset may not hold as data becomes more heterogeneous (different industries, company sizes, regions).
- Enterprise sales cycles are long; no evidence of sales capability yet.

**Conditional Terms Offered:**
1. **Revenue milestone:** €40K ARR by June 30, 2026 (convert 2+ pilots to paying customers at €1.5K+/month minimum).
2. **Funding release schedule:** First tranche €75K upon closing; second tranche €75K upon hitting €40K ARR milestone.
3. **Equity:** 10% (unchanged from ask).

**IC Discussion Note:**
Meeting-007 noted that the scoring matrix didn't handle conditional outcomes well. Tomás raised that MindBridge's decision was harder to quantify than others because success hinges on a specific near-term milestone. Suggest: **Process improvement for next funding round — add "conditional pathway" track to decision tree for companies that meet market/team criteria but lack revenue validation.**

**Letter Sent:** Conditional offer issued Nov 13, 2025. Founders have 2 weeks to respond with willingness to accept conditions.

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Await founder response (expected by Nov 27)
- [ ] If accepted: Term sheet drafting with milestone clauses
- [ ] If declined: Keep as warm lead; reapproach for future rounds

### 4. DataPulse (PASS)
**Founders:** Simone Marchetti (CEO), Elena Varga (CTO)
**Ask:** €350K at 5%, pre-money €6.65M
**Decision:** DECLINE

**Scoring Summary:**
- Product-Market Fit: 7/10 (€8K MRR from 2 customers; strong NPS 67; but claimed 50K MAU vs. earlier mention of 38K MAU — discrepancy noted)
- Team: 7.5/10 (Marchetti: 11 years e-commerce + founder mindset; Varga: ML background solid)
- Market Size: 7/10 (€127B fashion e-commerce TAM; but addressable to boutique/mid-market is narrower)
- Business Model: 7/10 (€4K/customer/month ACV reasonable; 74% gross margin strong)
- Execution Risk: Medium (retention unproven; only 2 customers; claimed growth numbers unverified)

**Rationale for Pass:**
- **Core concern:** Overvaluation relative to traction. €350K at 5% = €6.65M pre-money. At €8K MRR and modest churn assumptions, this values the company at 100x revenue — reasonable for SaaS but **only if retention holds.**
- **Data quality issue:** Pitch deck claims 50K MAU; analyst dossier notes "claimed 50K" but earlier data suggested 38K. This discrepancy raised red flag about data accuracy/transparency.
- **Customer concentration:** Only 2 customers. No evidence of sales motion beyond founder outreach. Customer success/retention risk is high.
- **Market fit unproven at scale:** Fashion e-commerce is competitive; competitors like Bloomreach have feature parity (and investor backing). DataPulse's differentiation (fit intent ML) is defensible but not yet defensible at enterprise scale.
- **Ask is high:** €350K dilution is significant for team that has only proven product-market fit with 2 customers.

**Feedback Provided:**
- DataPulse is a strong product and team. Encourage founders to re-approach after hitting 10 customers, €25K+ MRR, or demonstrable 2+ year retention curve.
- Current trajectory is positive (45% MoM growth). Revisit in 9 months with stronger metrics.
- Data transparency: If reapproaching, ensure MAU/CAC/LTV metrics are clearly documented and reconcilable.

**Decision Made:** Polite pass letter sent Nov 13, 2025.

3. **Operational execution** → Both investments today (NovaTech, UrbanFlow) selected partly based on strong product/operations execution signals (similar to NestEase pre-investment thesis)

## Process Observations

**Personal Note from Tomás Herrera, Analyst:**

This was Vertex Minds' first pitch day, and I believe the process worked well overall. The IC deliberation was thorough, decisions were well-reasoned, and each founder received substantive feedback (not just "yes" or "no").

**Observation on Process:** The scoring matrix helped structure discussion but had gaps. Specifically, conditional outcomes (like MindBridge) don't fit neatly into binary decision frameworks. The IC spent considerable time on MindBridge because the framework didn't account for milestone-based criteria well.

**Recommendation:** For next funding round, consider adding a "conditional pathway" track to the decision tree. This would allow the IC to quickly surface companies that are:
- Market/team validated (7+ on those dimensions)
- But revenue or customer validation incomplete
- Riskier than "pass," but lower conviction than "invest"

This would save deliberation time and clarify communication with founders (rather than debating whether to pass entirely on strong founders with weak traction).
