## Slide 2: The Problem
Construction & demolition (C&D) waste is a massive environmental and economic problem:
- EU generates 620 million tonnes of C&D waste annually
- 45% of waste is recyclable but ends up in landfills
- Contractors pay disposal costs (€60–120/tonne)
- Architects/developers want sustainable sourcing but no efficient marketplace exists
- Current process: fragmented, opaque, offline negotiations

**Market Inefficiency:** Millions in wasted materials + disposal costs + missed environmental targets.

---

## Slide 3: The Solution
GreenLoop is a B2B marketplace connecting:
- **Suppliers:** Demolition firms, construction contractors (listing surplus materials)
- **Buyers:** Architects, developers, contractors seeking reclaimed/surplus goods

**Core Features:**
- Verified supplier directory with material catalogs
- Real-time inventory matching (roof tiles, bricks, timber, fixtures, hardware)
- Pricing transparency + bulk discounts
- Logistics coordination (we don't move materials; match + facilitate)

## Slide 4: Market Opportunity
**TAM: €14B**
- EU C&D waste market: 620M tonnes/year
- Estimated 30% is potentially tradeable (€14B if monetized)
- Policy tailwinds: EU Circular Economy Action Plan, stricter landfill taxes

**SOM:** Germany, France, Italy, Spain (450M people, highest C&D density in EU)

---

## Slide 5: Business Model
- **Take Rate:** 8% of transaction value (buyer side)
- **Premium Listings:** €45/month (verified suppliers, featured placement)
- **Data & Insights:** Market reports (€2K–5K per report, future revenue)

**Unit Economics:**
- Average transaction: €2,400
- GreenLoop revenue per transaction: €192
- Target: 10K transactions/month (€1.92M GMV → €153K revenue)

## Slide 14: Environmental Impact
Every transaction on GreenLoop:
- Diverts 50–200 tonnes of material from landfill
- Saves ~2–5 tonnes of CO2 equivalent (vs. new material production)
- At 10K transactions/month: 50K–100K tonnes diverted, 200K+ tonnes CO2 saved annually

This aligns with EU waste directives and builder ESG commitments.
