# Vertex Minds LLC — Investment Thesis

## Who We Are

Vertex Minds is an AI-native venture studio based in Milan. We combine operational support with early-stage capital to help technical founders navigate the gap between prototype and product-market fit. Fund I is a €3.2M vehicle targeting 12–15 investments over 24 months.

## What We Look For

### The Non-Negotiable: Cash Flow Sustainability

Before anything else, we ask: can this business reach cash flow sustainability within a credible timeline, given the capital being raised?

This single filter eliminates roughly 90% of the startups we see. Most pre-seed decks show hockey-stick projections with no credible bridge between current burn and breakeven. We don't fund narratives — we fund mechanics.

A startup doesn't need to be profitable at the time of investment. It needs to demonstrate a path to unit economics that works, with assumptions we can stress-test.

### Three Assets We Underwrite

After the cash flow gate, we evaluate three assets:

**1. Team**
- Founders aged 26–50 with relevant industry experience (5+ years in domain OR prior startup experience with measurable outcomes)
- Technical co-founder is mandatory for any product with a technology moat claim
- We value complementary founding teams over solo founders
- Track record matters more than pedigree: we care about what you built, not where you studied
- Red flag: founder who can't explain unit economics without looking at the deck

**2. Technology**
- Defensible technical advantage: proprietary model, unique dataset, patent-pending IP, or deep integration moat
- We do NOT invest in thin wrappers around foundation models (GPT API + prompt engineering ≠ a tech company)
- Production-readiness matters: a working prototype with real users beats a research paper every time
- We assess technical debt honestly — a fast-shipped MVP with known debt is fine; a house of cards is not

**3. Traction**
- Revenue is the strongest signal. Even €1K MRR from paying customers changes the conversation entirely.
- For pre-revenue: LOIs, pilot agreements, waitlist with conversion intent, or a design partner with skin in the game
- Retention > acquisition: we'd rather see 50 users with 90% monthly retention than 5,000 signups with 10% activation
- Pipeline visibility: can the founder articulate their next 6 months of commercial activity without hand-waving?

### Scoring and Decision Framework

We use a 12-KPI weighted scoring model. The KPIs and their criticality weights:

| KPI | Weight |
|-----|--------|
| Cash flow sustainability path | Critical |
| CEO technical competency | High |
| Team track record | High |
| Founder age/experience fit | Medium |
| Technology defensibility | High |
| Commercial traction | High |
| Sector alignment with thesis | Medium |
| Scalability of model | Medium |
| Market opportunity size | Medium |
| Competitive advantage clarity | High |
| Exit pathway visibility | Low |
| Vertex Minds value-add potential | Medium |

Minimum score: 35/60. Ideal: 45/60.

We track every deal scored. After 12 months we'll back-test the model against portfolio performance and recalibrate.

## Sectors We Like

- **B2B SaaS** with vertical specialization (horizontal plays need exceptional traction)
- **AI/ML applications** solving measurable business problems (not "AI for AI's sake")
- **Marketplace models** with defensible supply-side acquisition
- **Regulatory technology** riding compliance tailwinds (EU AI Act, ESG reporting, customs reform)
- **Sustainability-adjacent tech** where the business model works without subsidies

## Sectors We Avoid

- Pure consumer/D2C (CAC dynamics are brutal and we lack operational expertise here — though we learned this the hard way, see FitBrew)
- Hardware-first with no software revenue layer
- Crypto/Web3 (not a conviction bet for this fund)
- Anything requiring regulatory approval >18 months before first revenue

## What We Bring Beyond Capital

- **Operational playbook**: sprint-based go-to-market support, product roadmap advisory, hiring frameworks
- **Technical resources**: architecture review, infrastructure optimization, data pipeline consulting
- **Network**: LP introductions for commercial partnerships, co-investor syndication for follow-on rounds
- **Governance**: board observer seat, monthly founder check-ins, quarterly portfolio reviews

## Rejection Red Flags

Things that make us say no regardless of other signals:
- Pre-revenue with post-money valuation >€5M (math doesn't work for our check size)
- Founder who outsources core product development
- TAM slide with a top-down number and no bottom-up validation
- "We'll monetize later" without a credible explanation of when and how
- Founder who is not coachable (we test this during screening calls)
- Team where nobody has direct domain experience in the target market
