## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NovaTech AI is a B2B SaaS platform automating technical documentation workflows for architecture and engineering firms across the EU. The founding team has built a domain-specialized NLP model trained on €2.1B TAM regulatory and technical frameworks to reduce the time architects and engineers spend on permit documentation, capitolati (technical specifications), and compliance reporting.

**Investment Ask:** €200,000 (8% equity, pre-money €2.3M)
**Recommendation:** **INVEST**

The company demonstrates early product-market fit signals with 12 studios in beta (3 paying), an estimated NRR of 140%, and a clear path to revenue. While competitive pressures and regulatory fragmentation present risks, the addressable market remains substantial and largely underserved. The founding team shows technical depth and operational competence.

## PRODUCT & TECHNOLOGY

### Core Offering

NovaTech AI provides an intelligent document generation and review platform for architectural and engineering workflows. The product integrates with firms' existing tools (Revit, AutoCAD, cloud storage systems) and uses NLP to:

1. **Auto-generate technical specifications** from BIM data and regulatory requirements
2. **Draft permit documentation** in compliance with regional building codes
3. **Perform compliance checks** against evolving standards
4. **Generate capitolati** (detailed technical and commercial specifications required in Italian/EU procurement)

### Technical Foundation

The core technology is a fine-tuned large language model trained on a proprietary dataset of ~200,000 architectural and engineering documents (permits, standards, specifications, precedents). The model was trained using transfer learning from a public base model, then adapted via supervised fine-tuning on this domain-specific corpus.

**Performance Claims:**
- Reduces documentation time from ~40 hours per project to ~4 hours
- Estimated accuracy on specification generation: 94% (internal evaluation)
- Model handles multi-language regulatory frameworks (Italian, German, French regulations for architectural permits)

**CRITICAL NOTE — Analyst Caveat:**
We were unable to independently verify NLP model performance claims. Accuracy benchmarks provided by the founding team only, derived from internal test sets. **Recommend requesting third-party evaluation before closing.** This is standard for AI-focused investments, but given the regulatory domain, independent validation of compliance-related accuracy is particularly important. Model failures in this space carry reputational and legal liability for customers.

### Data & Moat

The 200K document dataset is proprietary and sourced from (1) public regulatory databases, (2) partnerships with 2 architecture associations, and (3) client contributions. This dataset is their primary technical moat, though regulatory documents are increasingly public. A defensible advantage will come from accumulated user corrections and usage data as the install base grows.

### MVP Status & Roadmap

Current product is in beta. The platform is functional and being used by 12 studios. Near-term roadmap includes: (1) integration with Revit plugins, (2) expanded language/regulatory support for Spain and France, (3) automated compliance audit features.

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Direct Competitors

1. **BuildSpec (Munich-based)** — Similar document automation for German market. Launched 2023, ~€1.2M ARR estimated. Strong in specifications but weaker on permit generation. Build quality is solid. We believe BuildSpec is not well-optimized for Italian/French regulatory complexity.

2. **DocFlow AI (US startup, remote)** — Broader document automation targeting law and architecture. Less specialized. Founded 2022, Series A raised in 2024. Pricing higher, feature set broader but shallower. Unlikely to compete aggressively in EU market near term.

3. **Compliance+ (Italian, private)** — Very small competitor, compliance-focused, weak product. Low threat. Often mentioned by prospects but rarely chosen.

### Indirect Competitors

- Large BIM software vendors (Autodesk, Nemetschek) are adding native documentation features. This is the most credible threat long-term, though these vendors have historically been slow to innovate in niche verticals.
- Custom in-house solutions at large firms (Scandinavian firms especially have invested heavily in internal tooling).

### Competitive Advantage

NovaTech's advantages: (1) specialized model trained on the specific regulatory complexity of EU architecture, (2) founder credibility and relationships in the space, (3) nimbleness compared to large vendors, (4) early-mover advantage in EU market before larger competitors enter. Disadvantages: (1) small team, limited resources, (2) high customer acquisition costs, (3) regulatory fragmentation means feature parity across markets is difficult.

## RISK ASSESSMENT

### High-Risk Factors

1. **Regulatory Fragmentation:** Building codes vary by region, municipality, even district. This creates significant ongoing R&D burden. Scaling across markets is harder than standard SaaS.

2. **Large Vendor Threat:** Autodesk or Nemetschek could integrate similar capabilities within 2-3 years. This would quickly commoditize the feature and pressure margins.

3. **Model Validation Gap:** As noted above, we have not independently verified accuracy claims. A real-world failure (incorrect compliance advice leading to permit rejection or liability) could tank the company.

4. **Founder Execution Risk:** Marco is first-time founder (capital scale). Yara's experience is in research, not production systems. Team is small; loss of either founder would be highly disruptive.

5. **Market Education:** Architects and engineers are not accustomed to delegating compliance-critical work to software. Adoption will require significant education and trust-building.

### Medium-Risk Factors

1. **Pricing Sensitivity:** We don't yet know if €1,400/month is optimal. Willingness to pay may be lower than current pilots suggest, especially for smaller firms.

2. **International Expansion:** GTM in new countries will require local hiring and regulatory expertise. This is capital-intensive and distracts from core product.

3. **Data Privacy/GDPR:** Sentiment analysis and compliance tools may process sensitive project data. Regulatory compliance is mandatory. The team has not yet completed a full GDPR audit.

### Mitigating Factors

1. **Early Market Love:** 3 out of 12 pilots have converted to paying, and several others have expressed strong interest. This is solid for pre-product-market-fit.

2. **Experienced CTO:** Yara's PhD and ML research background significantly reduces technical execution risk. NLP domain specialization is exactly what this product needs.

3. **Clear Unit Economics:** Even at current small scale, LTV/CAC is above 4x, which is healthy.

4. **Differentiated Approach:** Unlike generic document automation platforms, NovaTech is betting on specialized regulatory expertise. If executed well, this is a defensible positioning.

## INVESTMENT CONCERNS & CONDITIONS

Before closing, we recommend:

1. **Third-Party AI Model Validation:** Commission independent accuracy audit of compliance generation on test set of 500+ documents. Cost ~€15K, timeline 4-6 weeks.
2. **Customer Reference Calls:** Direct calls with all 3 paying customers to verify unit economics and satisfaction claims.
3. **Legal Due Diligence:** Confirm IP ownership of the 200K document dataset. Ensure partnerships with architecture associations are properly documented.
4. **GDPR Audit Plan:** Require a detailed remediation plan for any gaps found in the compliance audit.
