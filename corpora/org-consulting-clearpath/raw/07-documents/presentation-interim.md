## Slide 4: ClearPath's Core Workflows

Three primary workflows identified:

1. **Onboarding & Project Setup**
   - New client engagement through team allocation and project plan
   - Average duration: 6–8 weeks
   - Quality variance: High

2. **Audit & Compliance Documentation Delivery**
   - Client audit request → scope assessment → documentation preparation → delivery → approval
   - Average cycle time: 5–8 days
   - Quality variance: Moderate; inconsistent across audit types

3. **Reporting & Analytics**
   - Project hours tracking → invoice preparation → client reporting
   - Average cycle time: 4–6 days per invoice
   - Pain point: Manual processes; limited analytics capability

## Slide 5: Onboarding Process – Current State

**Current workflow:**

```
Client Win
    ↓
Initial Scoping Meeting (1–2 days)
    ↓
Legal/Contracting (5–10 days; significant variance)
    ↓
Resource Assignment (3–5 days)
    ↓
Team Kick-off Meeting (1–2 days)
    ↓
Project Plan Development (5–10 days)
    ↓
Tool Setup & Access Provisioning (3–7 days)
    ↓
Team Onboarding & Knowledge Transfer (7–14 days)
    ↓
First Delivery Ready
```

**Average cycle time:** 36–55 days (5–8 weeks)

**Key findings:**
- Legal/contracting process is bottleneck (5–10 days; no published SLA)
- Resource assignment dependent on single person (Head of Ops); no backup
- Tool setup (VPN, email, document access) takes 3–7 days; ad hoc process
- Knowledge transfer done via unstructured 1:1 conversations; inconsistent
- Onboarding considered "ramp-up" activity; team utilization low during first 2–3 weeks

## Slide 6: Audit Process – Current State

**Current workflow:**

```
Client Audit Request
    ↓
Intake Meeting (1 day)
    ↓
Scope & Budget Assessment (1–2 days)
    ↓
Documentation Review & Gathering (1–3 days)
    ↓
Analysis & Finding Summary (2–5 days; HIGH VARIANCE)
    ↓
Report Draft (1–2 days)
    ↓
Client Review & Feedback (1–3 days)
    ↓
Final Report Delivery
```

**Average cycle time:** 7–16 days

**Key findings:**
- Analysis and finding summary is bottleneck and high-variance step
- Quality and thoroughness depends heavily on assigned auditor expertise
- Junior auditors require significant supervision from senior staff
- Limited templates or standardized approaches; largely bespoke per client
- Client feedback loop extends cycle time (and sometimes causes rework)

**Speaker Notes:**
This is where we're seeing the most pain from the team. Senior auditors are stretched thin; they're doing the complex audits AND reviewing junior auditor work. When a junior gets assigned an audit outside their expertise, cycle time extends significantly. We've observed cases where audits took 14+ days because the assigned person was unfamiliar with the client's sector or specific regulatory domain.

## Slide 7: Reporting Process – Current State

**Current workflow:**

```
Project End or Invoice Trigger
    ↓
Hours Consolidation (1–2 days; manual; multiple systems)
    ↓
Time Entry Review & Correction (1–2 days)
    ↓
Invoice Template Population (1 day; manual data entry)
    ↓
Client Report Preparation (1–3 days; manual document creation)
    ↓
Management Review & Approval (1–2 days)
    ↓
Client Delivery
```

**Average cycle time:** 5–10 days per invoice/report cycle

**Key findings:**
- Hours data captured in multiple systems (manual timesheets, separate project management tool with data not syncing automatically)
- Significant manual data entry and reconciliation required
- No real-time dashboard or analytics; management visibility limited
- Client reporting template-based but heavily customized per client
- Rework common due to data entry errors or incomplete time entry

**Speaker Notes:**
The finance/ops team is telling us this is one of the most time-consuming manual processes. Hours aren't flowing automatically from the project management tool to the billing system; someone has to manually pull them out, reconcile, and re-enter. We're seeing errors about 18% of the time that require corrections and rework.

## Slide 9: Key Data Findings

**Project Intake & Utilization:**
- Average utilization rate: 68% (benchmark: 75–80%)
- Onboarding period averages 2–3 weeks at <50% utilization per team member
- Projects with poor intake scoping have 12–15% rework (vs. 3–4% for well-scoped projects)

**Team Distribution:**
- Senior staff (10 FTE): 72% billable
- Intermediate staff (35 FTE): 71% billable
- Junior staff (35 FTE): 58% billable (onboarding + supervision overhead)

**Project Duration:**
- Small audits (<40 hours): 8–12 day average cycle time
- Medium projects (40–160 hours): 25–40 day cycle time
- Large engagements (160+ hours): 45–65 day cycle time

**Quality Metrics:**
- Audit rework rate: 8–12% (client feedback requiring revision)
- Invoice errors: 18% (requiring rework before client delivery)
- Project scope changes: 22% of projects (indicating intake quality issues)

## Slide 11: Root Cause Analysis – Preliminary Hypotheses

**Onboarding Bottleneck – Root Causes:**

1. **Organizational:** No published process; dependent on Head of Operations; no backup coverage
   - Implication: Scaling will require process formalization and team expansion
2. **Knowledge Management:** No structured knowledge base; new team members learn via 1:1 conversation
   - Implication: Each onboarding customized; variable quality
3. **Tool Integration:** Multiple systems not connected (CRM, project management, document storage)
   - Implication: Manual handoffs; delays in tool provisioning

**Audit Bottleneck – Root Causes:**

1. **Process Standardization:** Limited templates or standardized audit procedures
   - Implication: Each audit approached from first principles; variance in quality
2. **Expertise Distribution:** Audit quality/speed dependent on assigned person's sector knowledge
   - Implication: Junior staff unable to deliver independently; supervision overhead
3. **Quality Control:** Feedback loop unstructured; client revisions common
   - Implication: Cycle time extends; rework

**Reporting Bottleneck – Root Causes:**

1. **Systems Integration:** Time entry exists in 2+ systems; manual reconciliation required
   - Implication: Data accuracy issues; rework
2. **Process Automation:** Invoice and reporting generation largely manual
   - Implication: Time-consuming; prone to error

## Slide 12: Themes Emerging Across Workflows

Three cross-cutting themes:

1. **Knowledge Silos**
   - Critical regulatory and process knowledge resides with individuals
   - Limited documentation or templates
   - Scaling constrained by need to build expertise in new people individually

2. **Process Informality**
   - Most critical processes (intake, onboarding, audit) lack published procedures or SLAs
   - Ad hoc approaches; variance in quality and timing
   - Scaling difficult without standardization

3. **Systems Fragmentation**
   - Key workflows span multiple systems without integration
   - Manual handoffs; data entry errors; delays

**Implication:** These themes suggest ClearPath is at an organizational inflection point. Current model (small, expert-dependent team; informal processes) works at ~80 people but does not scale to 150–200 people.
