# Observation Note 001: New Consultant Onboarding Process

**Observation Date:** September 18, 2025
**Observer:** Yuki Tanaka (Meridian Labs)
**Location:** ClearPath Solutions Inc., Florence
**Duration:** ~4.5 hours (9:00 AM – 1:30 PM)
**Subject:** First-day onboarding for new compliance consultant (hire #56, Elena Martinelli)

---

## Overview

Attended the full onboarding day for Elena Martinelli, recently hired as a junior compliance consultant. Elena has background in regulatory affairs but is new to ClearPath's processes and client base. The onboarding sequence was primarily informal, with mentoring delegated to a senior consultant (Michele, ~3 years tenure) without structured documentation or formal checklist.

## Physical Setup & Initial Check-in

Elena arrived at 8:50 AM. No one was expecting her at reception — the receptionist had to call upstairs to confirm. She waited ~12 minutes for Michele to come down. During this time, Elena was not offered coffee or given any written materials about the company.

Michele brought her to a shared workstation in the open office. The desk had a monitor, keyboard, but no name placard or assigned space designation. No welcome kit, no org chart, no policy documents visible on the desk.

## IT Provisioning Issues

This is where significant friction emerged. Elena needed laptop access, email account, and client database credentials. IT department (single person, Roberto) was handling the request. Michele explained that IT typically takes 2-3 business days for full provisioning, but said there's usually a workaround.

By 10:30 AM, Elena had:
- Email address created (working)
- Laptop temporarily provided (loaner unit, 4-5 years old, slow)
- Database access still pending (Roberto said "probably tomorrow")

This meant Elena could not access client files or begin substantive work on her first day. Instead, she spent time reading printed regulatory guides from prior years (2022-2023, some content noted as "possibly outdated" by Michele).

## Mentoring & Knowledge Transfer

Michele walked Elena through:
1. Basic office protocols (kitchen location, parking, break schedule)
2. Overview of three active clients he manages
3. Internal filing structure (partially explained via shared drive)
4. A few recent compliance templates used on active projects

The explanations were conversational and practical, but not systematic. No written SOPs were referenced. When Elena asked about versioning practices for documents, Michele said "we usually just date things, but honestly it's a bit inconsistent across teams."

Michele did not have a prepared agenda or materials. He pulled up files on his screen and walked her through them informally. Duration was approximately 45 minutes, interrupted twice by client calls.

## Shadowing Activity

From 11:30 AM to 1:15 PM, Elena shadowed Michele on two client calls:
1. Status check with client compliance officer (Pharma Corp subsidiary)
2. Discussion of upcoming audit scope change

Elena was not briefed beforehand on the clients, their regulatory context, or what to listen for. She took notes but appeared uncertain about what information was critical. Michele did not debrief with her after the calls.

## Gaps & Observations

- No formal onboarding checklist or tracking document visible
- No formal introduction to broader ClearPath team (Elena met approximately 4 colleagues by chance/hallway conversation)
- No documentation of learning objectives or success criteria for first week
- Dependency on a single person (Michele) for all initial knowledge transfer
- No structured schedule for Elena's first week provided
- IT delays create passive time that could be spent on structured material

The process was functional but relied heavily on Michele's availability and goodwill. Scalability concerns emerged immediately: ClearPath is planning significant growth, and this model would strain experienced staff who would need to mentor multiple new hires simultaneously.

## Notes for Follow-up

- Recommend observing IT provisioning process separately
- May be useful to track how Elena's knowledge retention develops over first month
- Suggestion for interviews: ask other recent hires about their onboarding experience to identify if this is representative
