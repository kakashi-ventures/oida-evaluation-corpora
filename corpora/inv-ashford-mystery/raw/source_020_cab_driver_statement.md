TEXT:
I'd been parked at the Greyhound terminal waiting for a fare. The kid — I'd seen him around there for a few weeks, sleeping in the doorway some nights — he was sitting on the bench eating from a paper bag. There was a guy with him, standing. The guy was — average. Glasses, the small wire kind. Dark jacket. Soft voice; I couldn't hear them but the kid was nodding along and the guy wasn't gesturing. Could've been a teacher having a chat. I picked up a fare around eleven and when I came back twenty minutes later they were both gone. I didn't think anything of it. The kid would talk to anybody who'd buy him a sandwich.
