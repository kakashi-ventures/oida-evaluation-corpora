TEXT:
Daniel was a regular. He'd been on the pinball machines most of the evening of the 12th — the Bally one by the back wall. Around ten-thirty or so, a man came in. Older guy, tall, glasses, denim jacket. He watched Daniel play for a while, didn't put any tokens down himself. Then he went over and said something to Daniel at the change machine. I couldn't hear over the noise. Few minutes later they walked out together. I'd never seen the guy before. He didn't look like he belonged in an arcade. Time? Maybe ten-thirty. Could've been later. The clock by the door has been wrong for months — runs slow.
