TEXT:
Marcus had been coming in two or three nights a week since the new year. Quiet kid. Nursed a beer. Didn't bother anybody. The night of the eighth he was at the end of the bar talking to a regular — well, not really a regular, but a guy who'd been in maybe five or six times since fall. Tall, glasses, soft voice. He always ordered the same thing: rum and coke, extra ice, drank it slow. Paid cash both times I served him that night. They left together a little before one. I remember because I was about to call last call. I never caught the guy's name. He wasn't friendly enough to introduce himself, but he wasn't unfriendly either. He just wasn't there for the company.
