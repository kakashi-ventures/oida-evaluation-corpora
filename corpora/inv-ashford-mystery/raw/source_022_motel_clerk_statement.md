TEXT:
A gentleman came in around eleven-thirty on the fourth, asked for a single, paid cash for one night, signed the book "R. Albers." White, maybe early thirties, glasses — small ones, wire frames — dark jacket. Soft-spoken. The thing I remembered was he asked whether the room had good light. I said the lamp worked fine. He said no, he meant for photography. He said he did portraits sometimes and needed to see his work. At eleven-thirty at night, you know. I gave him Room 14. I never saw who else came in or went out. Wasn't my job to watch the lot.
