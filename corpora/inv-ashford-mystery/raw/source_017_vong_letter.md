TEXT:
Maya,
I'm writing because I can't stop thinking about it. I saw Eric on Friday the fifteenth, three days before. He told me he was finally going to do it — tell Patrick he had to be out by January first. He said he didn't care if it got ugly. I told him he could have the spare room in my apartment if he needed somewhere to land. He thanked me and said he hoped it wouldn't come to that. Patrick had been — I don't know how to say this without sounding like I'm accusing him, but Patrick had been frightening Eric. Eric had a bruise on his wrist in October and laughed it off. I should have said something.
Tess
