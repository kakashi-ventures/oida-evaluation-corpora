Internal note re: Halloran. Three interviews with Patrick Vail to date. The general shape of his account is consistent — argument, departed for Halbert's around midnight, returned at dawn — but the timing has shifted on each retelling. First interview placed his return at "around six." Second placed it at "five-thirty or six." Third placed it at "a little before six." Halbert's corroboration is consistent in shape but vague in detail; on second pressing he allowed that Vail "could have left after two." Manner of death and intimacy of attack (manual pressure, defensive wounds, bitten finger) point to a known associate. Absence of sedative and absence of theft are entirely inconsistent with the Reyes/Webb pattern. Recommend continued focus on Vail. Recommend explicit non-linkage to the rail-corridor cases in any internal or external communication.
