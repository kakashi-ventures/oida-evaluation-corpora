# Observation Note 002-v2: Client Intake Process — Team Alpha [DIVERGENT VERSION]

**Observation Date:** September 22, 2025
**Observer:** Nora Chen (Meridian Labs)
**Location:** ClearPath Solutions Inc., Florence
**Duration:** ~2.5 hours (2:00 PM – 4:30 PM)
**Subject:** Intake meeting for new compliance client (FinServe GmbH, financial services firm, ~200 employees)

---

## Context & Setup

Observed the intake process for a newly signed client, FinServe GmbH, a German financial services company seeking compliance documentation and internal audit readiness. This represents Team Alpha's responsibility under Marco Fiore (team lead). The intake was triggered by a sales handoff call that morning.

The meeting took place in ClearPath's main conference room. Attendees: Marco (team lead), Elena (junior analyst, same person from observation-001), and two representatives from FinServe (Petra Koch, Compliance Director; James Anderson, Operations Lead).

## Sales Handoff Process

No formal transition occurred before the intake meeting. Marco explained that the sales team typically calls him with basic details: company name, sector, budget range, and "what they think they need." For FinServe, this was communicated verbally in a 15-minute call that morning. No written brief was provided.

Marco's preparation consisted of:
- Opening a personal spreadsheet where he tracks active clients
- Pulling up ClearPath's standard service offerings (a PDF from 2024, slightly outdated)
- Locating his notes from a similar engagement last year

## Intake Meeting Conduct

Marco began without a formal agenda. The conversation was exploratory rather than structured. Topics covered:
- FinServe's current regulatory landscape (EU directives, specific sector rules)
- What documentation gaps they suspect
- Timeline expectations (3-4 month project target)
- Budget and resource availability

The meeting was conversational and relationship-building oriented. However, no standardized intake form was used. Marco took notes on his laptop, typing informally without a template.

Key information noted informally (no consistent formatting):
- "need audit-ready docs by Feb"
- "staff turnover is problem"
- "previous consultant left without handover"
- "budget about €45K"

## Post-Meeting Documentation

After the clients left, Marco returned to his personal spreadsheet and added a new row:
- Client name: FinServe GmbH
- Contact: Petra Koch (email noted in handwriting on a sticky note)
- Scope: "Audit readiness package" (vague)
- Timeline: "Feb deadline"
- Team: Marco, Elena, one analyst to be assigned

Marco did not create a formal project brief or engagement letter draft. He said that would come "later, when legal reviews it." No follow-up communication was scheduled with FinServe for clarification.

## Efficiency Observations

The entire intake consumed 2.5 hours:
- Client meeting: ~60 minutes
- Post-meeting documentation and planning: ~30 minutes
- Additional context-building (pulling old materials): ~40 minutes

A significant portion of time was spent searching for historical files and reconstructing context that could have been in a standardized brief. Marco mentioned needing to "dig through old projects" to find comparable engagements.

With a structured intake template, defined scope-setting questions, and pre-populated context from the sales handoff, this process could likely be completed in 45 minutes with comparable quality outcomes.

## Current Client Load

Marco is currently managing 5 active clients simultaneously at various project stages. He noted that onboarding at this pace is "manageable, but tight." This observation occurred before ClearPath's planned expansion to 150+ employees.

## Process Gaps

- No standardized intake form or template
- Minimal documentation of requirements
- Personal spreadsheet as the primary tracking system
- Dependency on verbal handoff from sales team
- No formal scope documentation until later legal review
- Search time for historical reference materials
- No clear criteria for success or deliverables definition at intake stage
- Possible misalignment between team lead perception and ops visibility on workload
