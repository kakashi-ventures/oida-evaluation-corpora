# Current-State Process Map: ClearPath Solutions Inc.

**Date Compiled:** October 14, 2025
**Compiled By:** Yuki Tanaka (Analyst, Meridian Labs LLC)
**Input From:** All team members, observation sessions, process interviews
**Format:** Narrative + step-by-step breakdown with responsible roles and estimated durations

---

## Overview

ClearPath Solutions operates across five primary process areas. This map documents the current-state workflow for each area, including steps, responsible roles, estimated cycle times, and identified pain points.

## Process Area 1: Client Intake & Scoping

**Responsible Lead:** Chiara Lentini (Client Relations)

### Steps

1. **Initial Client Contact**
   - Responsible: Chiara Lentini
   - Duration: 2–4 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Discovery call, requirements gathering, preliminary scope definition

2. **Scope Documentation**
   - Responsible: Chiara Lentini + Consultant assigned

3. **Internal Review**
   - Responsible: Priya Anand (Head of Ops) + David Park (CTO)
   - Duration: 1–2 days (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Budget alignment, resource availability, risk assessment

4. **Client Approval**
   - Responsible: Client, with Chiara managing communication
   - Duration: 3–7 business days (actual cycle time unknown)
   - Activity: Client reviews SOW, requests modifications, approves scope

5. **Engagement Kickoff**
   - Responsible: Chiara Lentini + assigned team leads
   - Duration: 4 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Team introduction, detailed timeline, communication protocols established

**Pain Points:**
- Scope creep frequently occurs after SOW approval
- No formal change request process
- Estimated durations are rough and inconsistent

## Process Area 2: Compliance Assessment & Data Collection

**Responsible Lead:** Laura Galli (Controls Assessment Lead)

### Steps

1. **Assessment Planning**
   - Responsible: Laura Galli + assigned analyst
   - Duration: 6–12 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Detailed assessment methodology, control matrix drafted, data collection checklist prepared

2. **Client Data Request**
   - Responsible: Consultant
   - Duration: 2 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Formal request issued to client for policies, logs, documentation

3. **Client Response & Data Provision**
   - Responsible: Client
   - Duration: 5–15 business days (actual cycle time unknown; client-dependent)
   - Activity: Client gathers and provides requested materials

4. **Data Review & Gap Analysis**
   - Responsible: Analyst assigned + Laura Galli (spot checks)
   - Duration: 16–32 hours per engagement (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Documents reviewed, control evidence assessed, gaps identified

5. **Preliminary Findings Discussion**
   - Responsible: Laura Galli
   - Duration: 1–2 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Call with client to clarify gaps, discuss remediation options

**Pain Points:**
- Client response times are unpredictable and often delayed
- Data is frequently incomplete, requiring follow-up requests (adds 3–5 business days per round)
- Analysts work with varying levels of detail in their reviews, leading to inconsistent quality
- No systematic tracking of what data has been received vs. requested

## Process Area 3: Analysis & Reporting

**Responsible Lead:** Riccardo Boni (Reporting & Documentation Lead)

### Steps

1. **Analysis Phase**
   - Responsible: Analyst assigned
   - Duration: 20–40 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Assessment data synthesized, findings documented, control assessments completed

2. **Draft Report Assembly**
   - Responsible: Analyst + Riccardo Boni
   - Duration: 8–16 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Report sections written/compiled, compliance matrix populated, recommendations drafted

3. **Internal Quality Review**
   - Responsible: Riccardo Boni
   - Duration: 3–6 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Grammar, formatting, technical accuracy checked; references verified

4. **Consultant Revision (if needed)**
   - Responsible: Analyst
   - Duration: 2–8 hours, if required (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Feedback incorporated, revisions made

5. **Final Report Assembly for Client**
   - Responsible: Riccardo Boni
   - Duration: 2–4 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Client names/dates finalized, formatting applied, executive summary prepared

**Pain Points:**
- Templates are fragmented across multiple locations (shared drive, email, Slack)
- Significant copy-paste activity from prior reports (60% of content in some cases)
- Quality inconsistency due to different analysts' approaches
- No systematic approach to ensuring all sections are updated with fresh analysis

## Process Area 4: Audit & Sign-Off

**Responsible Lead:** Marco Fiore (Audit Lead)

### Steps

1. **Audit Assignment**
   - Responsible: Marco Fiore
   - Duration: 1 hour (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Report assigned to available auditor, priority flagged

2. **Detailed Audit Review**
   - Responsible: Audit team member
   - Duration: 3–8 hours (estimated — factor-of-3 variation among team leaders; see observation-003)
   - Activity: Control logic verified, compliance findings reviewed, recommendations assessed for feasibility

3. **Auditor Markup & Feedback**
   - Responsible: Audit team member
   - Duration: 1–2 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Comments compiled, revision requests documented

4. **Consultant Revision**
   - Responsible: Original analyst
   - Duration: 2–6 hours (estimated — no measurement available)
   - Activity: Feedback addressed, revisions incorporated, resubmitted

5. **Final CTO Sign-Off**
   - Responsible: David Park (CTO)
   - Duration: 20–40 minutes per report (David's estimate)
   - Activity: Final review, approval, or rejection with comments

**Pain Points:**
- No tracking of time spent in audit queue; cycle time varies by factor of 3
- Rework occurs due to external standard changes not being tracked (see observation-003)
- Queue depth creates bottleneck at CTO sign-off stage (14 items observed waiting)
- No visibility into which reports are in which stage of the audit process
- Feedback from auditors is sometimes lost (consultant must recreate from memory on resubmission)

## Critical Data Limitations

- **Duration estimates are not based on actual time tracking.** Most durations are provided verbally by team leaders and represent professional judgment rather than measured data.
- **No system currently records when work moves between process stages**, making cycle time measurement impossible without manual logging.
- **Variation in estimates** (e.g., audit review time varying by factor of 3) suggests either inconsistent execution or inconsistent understanding of the process.
- **Client-dependent steps** (data provision, approval) are largely outside ClearPath's control and are not being tracked systematically.

---

**Recommendation**: Implement time tracking (at minimum: stage entry/exit dates) for a 2-week pilot to establish baseline cycle time data.
