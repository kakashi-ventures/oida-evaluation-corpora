[10:30] Nora: Moving to the second major area we've focused on—the audit queue. We've been watching that carefully because it was cited as a significant constraint. What we found is that it's not fundamentally a capacity problem. You have enough people. It's a process design problem.

[10:33] Thomas: What do you mean?

[10:34] Nora: The way the workflow is structured right now, junior consultants complete their audit work, and then it goes into a queue waiting for a senior person to do a review. While it's sitting in that queue—and we're seeing queues of fifteen to twenty items on average—the junior consultant is idle. They can't take new assignments because they're waiting for their work to be reviewed and have feedback. This creates a bottleneck that then cascades. The queue grows, wait times extend, and you see reduced throughput on the junior consultant side.

[10:37] Priya: But we do need that review step. It's a compliance requirement. We can't skip quality gates.

[10:38] Nora: Absolutely. We're not suggesting you skip review or compromise quality. We're suggesting you change the mechanism. Instead of a hard serial bottleneck where everything funnels through one review stage, there are parallel approaches that maintain rigor and actually strengthen it while accelerating throughput.

[10:41] Thomas: Such as?

[10:42] Marcus: Several options. One is structured peer review—instead of only senior staff doing review, consultants at similar levels review each other's work against a detailed checklist, and senior people do spot checks and quality audits on a percentage of items rather than reviewing every single one. You maintain quality through the rigor of the checklist and the spot-check sample, but you remove the serial bottleneck. Another approach is to partition the review gates. Instead of one big review at the end, you have smaller review points at different stages. That way feedback happens earlier, rework is smaller, and the final gate moves faster. There are other variants too. We'll want to get your compliance team involved to validate that these approaches meet your regulatory requirements, but I'm fairly confident there's substantial room to improve here without taking on additional compliance risk.

[10:48] Thomas: Okay. I'll definitely want to see detailed options and trade-offs in the final report.
