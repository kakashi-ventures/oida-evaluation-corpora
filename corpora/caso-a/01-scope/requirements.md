# Requirements — ClearPath Process Review

**Collected during:** Discovery phase, Weeks 1–3
**Compiled by:** Yuki Tanaka
**Last updated:** September 29, 2025

---

## Source

These requirements were gathered from the project brief, kickoff meeting, and initial stakeholder interviews (Thomas Reed, Priya Anand, David Park, plus 4 team leaders). They are listed as-received, without prioritization or feasibility assessment. Some are deliberately vague — the discovery phase is expected to sharpen them.

---

## R1. Onboarding

- **R1.1** Reduce time-to-independence for new consultants from the current ~6 weeks to 3 weeks or less.
- **R1.2** Create a standardized onboarding checklist that applies across all teams, while allowing team-specific modules.
- **R1.3** Formalize the mentorship pairing process — currently it's "whoever has capacity," which often means nobody.
- **R1.4** Ensure new hires have access to all required systems and templates on Day 1. Priya noted that IT provisioning currently takes 3–5 days post-start.
- **R1.5** Develop a "readiness assessment" mechanism — currently there's no formal check on whether a new consultant can work independently. It's a subjective call by the team leader.

## R2. Client Intake & Scoping

- **R2.1** Standardize the client intake process across teams. Currently each of the team leaders (4? 6? — conflicting information received) has their own approach.
- **R2.2** Create a unified intake template that captures all information needed for scoping, resourcing, and pricing.
- **R2.3** Establish clear handoff criteria from sales to delivery — multiple interviewees cited this as a pain point without specifying what exactly breaks down.
- **R2.4** Improve internal communication between teams during the intake phase. *(Note: this was raised by Thomas Reed in the brief but remains undefined. What does "improve" mean? Frequency? Channels? Content? To be explored.)*
- **R2.5** Reduce the average time from initial client contact to kickoff. Current estimate: 3–4 weeks. Target: 2 weeks. (Priya's estimate; David thinks the current timeline is fine and that rushing intake causes downstream quality issues.)

## R3. Audit & Quality Workflow

- **R3.1** Reduce audit queue backlog from 5–8 business days to ≤2 business days.
- **R3.2** Identify the specific stage(s) where the audit bottleneck occurs — current assumption is review-and-sign-off, but this is unverified.
- **R3.3** Define clear SLAs for internal audit turnaround.
- **R3.4** Explore whether a tiered review system could help (e.g., senior-only review for complex deliverables, peer review for routine ones).
- **R3.5** Reduce dependency on David Park as final sign-off authority — he currently reviews ~40% of all deliverables personally, which doesn't scale.

## R4. Reporting

- **R4.1** Standardize client report templates. Current state: each consultant has their own Excel-based templates, many of which contain outdated formatting or broken formulas.
- **R4.2** Reduce time spent on report assembly. Estimated current state: senior staff spend 15–20% of time on manual report preparation. Target: reduce by at least half.
- **R4.3** Explore centralized data sources for report generation — currently data is pulled manually from multiple systems (CRM, project tracker, email).
- **R4.4** Ensure reporting consistency for international clients who require English-language deliverables.

## R5. Knowledge Management & Sharing

- **R5.1** Reduce risk of knowledge loss when consultants leave or are reassigned. Three departures in the last year caused measurable disruption, though "measurable" was described only anecdotally — no data exists on recovery cost or time.
- **R5.2** Create some form of knowledge repository — wiki, shared drive, internal portal — where consultants document their regulatory expertise, client-specific learnings, and methodological insights.
- **R5.3** Implement regular knowledge-sharing sessions. *(Note: Priya mentioned that "we tried brown-bag lunches two years ago and nobody came." Need to understand why before recommending the same approach.)*
- **R5.4** Improve cross-team visibility — teams don't know what other teams are working on, which leads to duplicated research and missed opportunities for collaboration.
- **R5.5** Formalize the process for updating internal regulatory knowledge when laws change. Currently this is done informally by whichever consultant notices first.

## R6. Cross-Cutting / General

- **R6.1** Any process changes must be implementable without a major technology investment. Budget for tools is limited to what's within the existing €380K IT annual budget.
- **R6.2** Recommendations should be phased — the organization cannot absorb a complete process overhaul simultaneously.
- **R6.3** The roadmap must be presentable to the board by early December 2025.
- **R6.4** Changes should account for a bilingual working environment (Italian primary, English for international).
- **R6.5** The final deliverable should include estimated implementation effort per recommendation (person-weeks, not just priority labels).
